Acts 21

But as it happened, we were drawn away from them to put to sea, and sailed directly to Cos, and then on to Rhodes the next day, and from there to Patara.

And finding a ship crossing to Phoenicia, we boarded it and set sail.

But after sighting Cyprus and passing it on the port side we sailed to Syria and came to harbor at Tyre, for that was where the ship was unloading its cargo.

We stayed there for seven days after finding the disciples, and they, speaking through the spirit of God, told Paul not to embark for Jerusalem.

And when it came about that we'd finished our days, we departed and travelled on, with all those accompanying us with wives and children until out of the city, and then we knelt on the shore and offered up prayers.

We took leave of each other, and we boarded the ship, while they returned to their own homes.

And when we'd ended our sea-voyage from Tyre we reached Ptolemais, and after greeting our brothers we stayed one day with them.

And on the next day we left there and set out for Caesarea, and entered the house of Philip the evangelist, one of the seven, and stayed with him.

Now this man had four maiden daughters who prophesied.

And while we were staying for some days a prophet, named Agabus, came from Judea.

And when he came to us he took Paul's belt and bound his own feet and hands and said, "The Holy Spirit says this: In Jerusalem, the Jews will bind the man whose belt this is in this way, and they will hand him over into the hands of Gentiles.

And when we heard this, both we and the local men urged him not to go to Jerusalem.

Then Paul replied, "What you are doing, crying and breaking my heart? For I am ready not only to be bound but even to be killed in Jerusalem for the sake of the name of our lord Jesus."

And when he was not persuaded we fell silent, saying: "Let the Lord's will be done."

After those days we got ready and went to Jerusalem.

And some of the disciples from Caesarea went with us too, bringing along a certain Mnason, a Cypriate, a long-time disciple with whom we could stay.

And when we reached Jerusalem the brothers welcomed us joyfully.

And on the next day, Paul went with us to James, and all the elders were there.

And after greeting them he explained in turn each of the things God had done among the nations through his ministry.

Those who heard him praised God, and they said to him, "You see, brother, how many myriads of the faithful there are among the Jews, and they are all followers of the law.

They've been told about you that you teach all the Jews throughout the nations to revolt from Moses, telling them not to circumcise their children and not to walk according to the customs.

And so what is it? They will certainly hear that you have come.

So do what we tell you: we have four men who have laid a vow on themselves.

Take these men and purify yourself with them and pay for them to shave their heads, and everyone will realize that what they have been told about you is nothing, but that you yourself are following and keeping the law.

Concerning the Gentiles who are believers, we have sent off a letter, after deciding that they should keep away from this (circumcision?), as well as meats offered to idols, blood, strangled things and fornication.

Then Paul took the men and, on the following day, was purified with them and entered the temple, proclaiming the fulfillment "of the days of the purification" until the offering of each of them was offered.

As the seven days were about to come to an end, the Jews from Asia who saw him in the temple stirred the whole crowd up and laid their hands on him,

crying out: "Israelite men! Help us! This is the man who teaches everyone everywhere contrary to the people and the law and this place. What's more, he also brought Greeks into the temple and defiled this holy place."

For, since they'd seen Trophimus the Ephesian in the city with him, they thought that Paul had brought him into the temple.
